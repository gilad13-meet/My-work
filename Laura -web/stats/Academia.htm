Academia


Laura Wharton received a bachelor's degree in government from Harvard University, a master's degree in Russian and Slavic studies from the Hebrew University and a Ph.D. from the Hebrew University. Her doctoral thesis was on ideological changes in the Israeli Labor Party regarding socioeconomic issues between the years 1965-1977, advised by Prof. Itzhak Galnoor and Prof. Shlomo Avineri. Over the course of her studies she received numerous prizes and scholarships, including a joint scholarship from the Bela and Baruch Tal Foundation, a Yad Tabenkin Scholarship for outstanding doctoral students, a scholarship from the Levi Eshkol Institute for Social, Economic, and Political Research in Israel, a travel grant from the Association for Israel Studies, and a three-year scholarship of the Hoffman Program for Leadership and Social Responsibility for outstanding doctoral students at the Hebrew University. Upon finishing her doctorate in 2012 she began teaching as an external lecturer in the political science department of the Hebrew University. In 2013-2014 she will be co-teaching a course on religion and politics with Dr. Netanel Fisher.
